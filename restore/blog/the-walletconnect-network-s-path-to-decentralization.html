The WalletConnect Network’s Path to Decentralization

WalletConnect

Foundation

Exploring the Journey Toward Decentralization and why it matters

Centralization, while effective for rapid decision-making, maintaining control and central direction, has presented significant downfalls as the internet evolved over the years, such as lack of transparency and lack of cooperation. These risks are sometimes shown in web3, where users demand openness, security, and trustless environments. For infrastructure like the WalletConnect Network, these centralized flaws threaten the very opportunities that web3 and WalletConnect promises: decentralized ownership, control, and interoperability.

Decentralization can provide enhanced security, transparency, and autonomy, enabling an ecosystem where innovation can thrive. It's also where networks, projects and ecosystems can build on top of each other and learn collectively. The WalletConnect Network was built with decentralization in mind from day one, though from the beginning, it relied on a centralized structure for practical reasons and to help get it to a stable area of growth (As you can see today). From the very beginning, there has always been a roadmap designed to ensure the network's evolution would become a decentralized powerhouse that will enable wallets to interact seamlessly.

So what does this journey look like? And what does the end goal for WalletConnect Network look like?

Step 1: Initial Centralized Infrastructure (The beginning of the network)

In the beginning, WalletConnect started with a centralized architecture for efficiency, control and scalability. This decision enabled rapid iteration on the product, ensuring that it could scale and meet the needs of developers and users. Also, by staying centralized in this period it lead to huge growth and adoption of the product. By maintaining control over the network’s early stages, the WC team was able to implement key features like low-latency communication between wallets and dApps, ensuring a smooth UX (What WalletConnect is known for).

The benefit of this centralized start was clear: rapid deployment, seamless scaling, and the ability to manage key updates. However, centralization comes with trade-offs, particularly in the decentralized web3 space.

Step 2: Introduction of Permissioned Nodes (Mid 2024)

The first step toward decentralization came with the introduction of permissioned nodes. These nodes were operated by a small, trusted group of ecosystem partners (Including Ledger, Kiln, Luga Nodes, Sensei Nodes, Consensys, 1kx, Figment, Everstake, Arc Community, Nansen, Chain Safe, HexTrust, NodeMonster, QuantNode and CryptoMind), reducing the single-point-of-failure risk. This move improved resilience and distributed control among multiple entities.

For the WalletConnect Network, this was the first real taste of decentralization. More entities participating in network operation resulted in increased transparency and security, as multiple stakeholders held the responsibility of maintaining the network. Users and developers alike benefited from the stronger, more secure infrastructure. With plans to expand Service Nodes and improve their performance on the network (See page 8-9 of the Whitepaper).

Step 3: Transition to a Token-Governed Network (Late 2024)

With the launch of the WalletConnect Token (WCT), WalletConnect took a critical step toward decentralization. Governance decisions began shifting from the WalletConnect team to the wider community, allowing more collaboration and cooperation with WCT holders playing a central role in making decisions about the network’s direction and operation.

This shift unlocked community participation, empowering token holders to influence how the network evolves. For developers, this opened up a range of possibilities, as the network's future was no longer determined by a single entity but by the collective vision of its users. It also fostered greater trust within the ecosystem, since stakeholders had a voice in key decisions.

Step 4: Full Permissionless Network (Post-2025)

The ultimate phase of WalletConnect’s decentralization journey is the move to a fully permissionless network. By removing any centralized control over node operation, the network will become truly decentralized. Anyone who meets the necessary criteria will be able to run a node, further distributing power and ensuring no single entity has outsized influence.

This evolution creates a self-sustaining, open ecosystem. The benefits of this shift extend to the entire onchain universe: developers gain access to a decentralized infrastructure that scales, users trust a more secure network, and the ecosystem will grow and improve through governance such as DAO votes.

Outcome: A Fully Decentralized WalletConnect Network and stronger onchain ecosystem

Eventually, WalletConnect will transition into a fully decentralized protocol, allowing the entire ecosystem to operate without centralized intervention. The decentralization of WalletConnect Network ensures its infrastructure is resilient, scalable, and transparent. This will strengthen not only the WalletConnect network but also the broader web3 ecosystem across all chains and all projects.

The journey toward decentralization is not just a technical transition; it’s a philosophical one. It’s about ensuring that the future of the WalletConnect Network is shaped by its community and not by a central entity. This marks a pivotal moment in the evolution of decentralized networks, and WalletConnect is proud to lead the way, offering a glimpse into the future of a truly decentralized web3 ecosystem.
